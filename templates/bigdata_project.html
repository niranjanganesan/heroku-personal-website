Big Data Analysis on Ethereum Transactions and Smart Contracts

Cryptocurrency serves to be an important digital asset designed to work as a medium of exchange that uses strong cryptography to secure financial transactions, verify the transfer of assests and much more. The powerful Blockchain technology proved to be an strong alternative to digital currencies with its decentralized control. Ethereum right from its inception in 2015 has been one of the popular infrastructure for crypto payments.

Ethereum is a blockchain based distributed computing platform where users may exchange currency (Ether), provide or purchase services (smart contracts), mint their own coinage (tokens), as well as other applications. The Ethereum network is fully decentralised, managed by public-key cryptography, peer-to-peer networking, and proof-of-work to process/verify transactions.

Project Scope

The objective of this project is to analyse the full set of transactions which have occured on the Ethereum network;from the first transactions (14-02-2016) till 30-06-2019 using Big data tools such as Hadoop and Spark. The parallel computing architecture is the suitable method to analyze big datasets.

The following analysis are carried out in this project

⚫ Time Analysis - Number of transactions occuring every month from Feb 2016 to June 2019.

⚫ Top 10 Most Popular Services - Evaluation of top 10 smart contracts by total Ether received.

⚫ Comparative Evaluation of Hadoop and Spark.

⚫ Exploratory Data Analysis - Identifying the most lucrative form of scam and its changes over time. Time series analysis of gas price change over the years in Wei and USD. Time series analysis of Gas consumed by smart contratcs over the years.

Data Sources

The datasets required from the project are downloaded from the Ethereum dumps uploaded daily into a repository o Google BigQuery. A subset of these datasets are downloaded and stored in HDFS

2. Top 10 Smart Contracts by total Ether received

The top 10 most popular services is obtained by performing three different operations on the available data. First an Hadoop MRJob based approach is carried out to calculate the aggregate of total number of transactions that took place. The next step is to perform a repartition join between the aggregate(output of job 1) and contracts dataset. The join operation is performed on the transaction address field. This is to filter out only the smart contract address and reject the user address.

The third and final step is to filter out the top 10 smart contract services from the output dataset of previous step. A simple MRJob based approach to filter the top 10 records is performed on this dataset. Below are the top 10 smart contracts.

3. Exploratory Data Analysis

This section of the project is to dig more deep into the available datasets and present more meaningful insights. A variety of exploratory data analysis has been carried out such as Identifying the most lucrative form of scam and it changes over time, Time series analysis of gas price change over the years and its equivalent price in USD. Also, time series analysis of gas consumed by smart contracts over the years.

Most lucrative form of Scam

To identify the most lucrative form of scam I have joined the scams dataset and transactions dataset based on address of transaction as key. From the joined dataset the aggregate of values with scam category as key is calculated. From the aggregate output the scam category with highest values is the most lucrative form of scam.

Time series analysis of gas price change over the years(in Wei)

To calculate the gas price change over years the aggregate of gas price over time is used from the transactions dataset. The average value of gas price per month gives the time series analysis of how the price has changed over the years.

A MRJob based approach is used to obtain the result. The obtained results from the map reduce job is plotted using excel and below is the time series analysis graph of gas price change over years.

Time series analysis of gas price change over the years(in USD)

Data preprocessing is done the results from previous operation is converted into USD(from Wei). The exchange rate historical data was gathered from Etherscan The exchange rate values are stock rates per day and the rates vary with a single day until stock closes. Since the previous calculations in Wei are the average results per month, the average of USD prices per month is calculated to do the conversion. Another MRJob based approach is carried out to calculate the average of USD prices per month.
